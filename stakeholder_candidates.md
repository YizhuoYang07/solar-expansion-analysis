## Context: What Makes a Good Stakeholder?

The data analysis reveals that the global solar industry is at an inflection point:
- PV module costs are down **99.8%** since 1975 (now $0.258/W)
- Global solar generation crossed **2,094 TWh** in 2024 (up 12× since 2015)
- MENA markets (Saudi Arabia, Oman, Egypt) have **exceptional solar resources** (PVOUT >5.0 kWh/kWp/day) but **<4% solar share** — a structural gap

The ideal stakeholder is a company that:
1. Is actively deciding *where* to expand — so the data is actionable
2. Has the profile to operate in international markets
3. Has a clearly framed strategic question our data can answer

---

## Candidate 1 (RECOMMENDED): Canadian Solar Inc. (CSIQ)

**Type:** Solar module manufacturer + utility-scale project developer  
**HQ:** Guelph, Ontario, Canada (NASDAQ: CSIQ)  
**2023 Revenue:** ~$7.2 billion USD  
**Operations:** Manufacturing in China, SE Asia; projects in 50+ countries  

### Why Canadian Solar?

Canadian Solar's subsidiary **Recurrent Energy** is a global utility-scale solar developer actively seeking project opportunities in emerging markets. The company explicitly names MENA and Latin America as growth regions in its investor presentations.

**Strategic Question the data answers:**
> *"Which three international markets should Canadian Solar's Recurrent Energy division prioritise for utility-scale project development in 2025–2030, and why?"*

### How Our Data Supports This

| Data Insight | Business Relevance for Canadian Solar |
|---|---|
| Saudi Arabia: PVOUT 5.16, CAGR +108%, only 1.8% solar share | Clear #1 new market — Vision 2030 provides policy tailwind |
| Oman: PVOUT 5.17, CAGR +102%, low competition | Early-mover advantage in a growing Gulf market |
| Egypt: PVOUT 5.25, large electricity demand | Large addressable market, established FiT scheme |
| Colombia: CAGR +90%, Latin America presence | Leverage existing LatAm supply chain relationships |
| PV price now $0.258/W | All target markets now financially viable for utility-scale |
| Chile: 22% solar share at same PVOUT as Saudi Arabia | Proves MENA markets can replicate Chile's success |

### Narrative Arc (Detective Story)

- **Anomaly identified:** Egypt and Saudi Arabia have virtually identical solar physics to Chile — but Chile is at 22% solar share while they're at 2–4%. Why?
- **Clues follow:** It's not the sun. PV costs are universal. The gap is *investment, policy, and grid infrastructure* — all addressable.
- **Resolution:** The moment has arrived. Vision 2030 in Saudi Arabia, the Egypt FiT scheme, and Colombia's renewable auctions have removed the policy barrier. Canadian Solar should move now, before competition intensifies.

## Candidate 2: First Solar Inc. (FSLR)

**Type:** Solar module manufacturer (thin-film CdTe technology) + project developer  
**HQ:** Tempe, Arizona, USA (NASDAQ: FSLR)  
**2023 Revenue:** ~$3.3 billion USD  
**Focus:** Utility-scale, high-irradiance environments (MENA is a perfect fit for CdTe)

### Why First Solar?

First Solar's CdTe thin-film modules perform **better than conventional silicon** in high-temperature, high-irradiance conditions — exactly the conditions in the MENA region where GHI exceeds 6.0 kWh/m²/day. They are actively expanding manufacturing in India and the Middle East.

**Strategic Question:**
> *"Which markets globally offer the strongest case for First Solar's thin-film technology advantage, and what is the total addressable market if those countries reached 15% solar share?"*

### Key Differentiator vs Candidate 1

First Solar is a *technology differentiation* story — the narrative would focus on *where their product performs best* (MENA, Africa). Canadian Solar is an *expansion opportunity* story — broader applicability to multiple markets.

**First Solar is a stronger fit if your group wants to focus on:**
- The physics angle (GHI, temperature coefficients)
- Market sizing calculations (GW × price = revenue)
- The US Inflation Reduction Act as a boost to their US manufacturing

## Candidate 3: Masdar (Abu Dhabi Future Energy Company)

**Type:** State-owned renewable energy company  
**HQ:** Abu Dhabi, UAE  
**Ownership:** ADNOC (70%), Mubadala Investment Company (20%), ADQ (10%)  
**Scale:** 20+ GW clean energy capacity globally

### Why Masdar?

Masdar is the UAE's flagship renewable energy developer and has stated a target of **100 GW by 2030**. They are already present in Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Uzbekistan, UK, and 40+ countries. The data directly supports their expansion roadmap.

**Strategic Question:**
> *"Our data confirms that the MENA region's untapped solar potential is larger than its entire current installed base. Where should Masdar focus its next five international partnerships?"*

### Consideration

Masdar is a good choice if your group wants to emphasise the **government/sovereign wealth fund** angle and the **geopolitical context** of energy transition in oil-producing nations. However, as a private (state-owned) company, there is less public financial data available for cross-referencing.

## Recommendation

**Primary recommendation: Canadian Solar (CSIQ)**

Reasons:
1. The broadest geographic story — covers MENA AND Latin America, giving richer visualisation options
2. They are both a manufacturer AND developer — so you can use the PV price data AND the market opportunity data
